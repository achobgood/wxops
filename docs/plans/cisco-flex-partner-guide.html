What Is the Flex Plan?

The Cisco Collaboration Flex Plan 3.0 is Cisco's subscription licensing model for their entire collaboration portfolio. It converts traditional capex (perpetual licenses + SmartNet) into predictable opex with monthly per-user pricing.

Partner Tip
Customers can purchase individual products (just Calling, just Meetings) or bundles (Webex Suite). They can also run cloud and on-premises calling simultaneously during a migration — Cisco offers an 18-month transition license for this.

Section 04

True Forward — How It Works

True Forward is the mechanism Cisco uses to reconcile usage in Enterprise Agreement subscriptions. It only applies to the EA buying model — Named User customers simply order the quantities they need.

True Forward vs. Traditional True-Up

Traditional True-Up
“You used more → pay us for the past overage.”
Retroactive billing for the entire period of overconsumption.
Cisco True Forward
“You used more → your new baseline goes up going forward.”
No retroactive billing. Past overconsumption is effectively free.

True Forward Timeline

Year 1 Start
Customer signs EA for 500 Calling Pro licenses.
Buffer: up to 575 (500 + 15%)
During Year 1
Customer grows to 600 users. Exceeds 575 buffer. No extra charge during the year.
Anniversary (TFAD)
True Forward evaluated. New baseline = 600. Pays for 25 additional users × remaining term.
Year 2+
New buffer: up to 690 (600 + 15%). Cycle repeats at next anniversary.

Step by Step

  1. Customer signs EA for 500 Webex Calling Professional licenses
  2. Growth allowance kicks in — customer can use up to 575 licenses (500 + 15%) without triggering True Forward
  3. During the year, customer grows to 600 users (exceeds the 575 growth buffer)
  4. ~30 days before anniversary, Cisco’s automated system in CCW compares Control Hub consumption data against entitlements
  5. At the anniversary (TFAD): Overconsumption detected (600 vs 575 allowed). New committed quantity = 600. Customer pays: Unit Price × 25 users × Remaining months
  6. New growth allowance recalculates: 600 + 15% = 690 users before next True Forward
What If They Stay Within the Buffer?
If the customer grows from 500 to 560 users (within the 575 buffer), nothing happens. No True Forward event. No additional charges. The committed quantity stays at 500.
Can the Customer Ever Go Down?
No. The entitlement can only increase. Even if the customer drops from 600 back to 400 users, they remain committed to the 600-user baseline through the end of the term. This is not negotiable mid-term.

Intra-Suite Value Shift

If the customer has a Webex Suite EA (Calling + Meetings + Messaging) and they’re over on Calling but under on Meetings, the residual value of unused Meetings licenses automatically offsets the Calling overage.

  • Happens automatically — no partner action needed
  • Calculated on purchase value, not license count
  • Only works within the same suite

Section 05

Purchasing Flow for Partners

Prerequisites

  • Partner must be SaaS Subscription Resale certified
  • Access to Cisco Commerce Workspace (CCW)
  • Familiarity with CCW Annuity platform

Step-by-Step Ordering

A2Q (Approval to Quote)

Submit an Approval to Quote request through the A2Q portal before creating any order. Required for initial purchases, design changes, and adding new suites.

Do not skip this step. Orders without A2Q may be delayed or rejected.

Create Estimate in CCW

Start a new Flex Plan estimate using the top-level SKU A-FLEX. Select buying model, products, tiers, term length, and billing frequency.

Build Configuration

Select quantities, add-ons (PSTN, devices, Customer Assist), promotions/incentives. For EA, define the Knowledge Worker count.

Generate Quote

CCW applies push promotion discounts. Choose incentives (OIP hunting/teaming) via “Create a Deal.”

Attach Required Documents

Attach the signed End User Information Form (EUIF) and A2Q approval to the order. Documents on the quote do NOT flow to the order — re-attach at order time.

Submit for Approval

Submit the configured order for Cisco approval.

Convert to Order

Once approved, convert the quote to a live order in CCW.

Provisioning Begins

Customer’s Webex org is provisioned with purchased licenses. Licenses appear in Control Hub.

Section 07

Change / Modify / Grow

Mid-Term Additions (Most Common)

  1. Partner opens “Modify My Subscription” in CCW
  2. Select the active subscription to modify
  3. Add quantities (new users, new license tiers, new add-ons)
  4. New licenses are co-terminated with the original order by default
  5. Customer pays the prorated amount for the remaining term
  6. An A2Q post-sale review may be required for significant design changes

What You CAN Change Mid-Term

  • Add more licenses (same tier)
  • Add a new license tier
  • Add new products (e.g., Meetings to Calling)
  • Add new add-ons (PSTN, devices, CX)
  • Upgrade buying model (NU → EA)

What You CANNOT Change Mid-Term

  • Reduce committed license quantities
  • Downgrade buying model (EA → NU)
  • Downgrade license tier (Pro → Standard)
  • Cancel the subscription

Co-Termination Rules

  • All mid-term additions automatically co-terminate with the initial order
  • If less than 12 months remain, a new separate purchase is required (cannot co-terminate)
  • You can opt out of co-termination for a new suite, creating a separate subscription with its own term

Section 08

Renewals

Auto-Renewal Notice
Flex Plan subscriptions auto-renew unless the customer or partner provides written notice at least 45 days before the end of the current Use Term.

Renewal Process

  1. Use “Renew Your Subscription” in CCW
  2. Changes (quantities, billing frequency, added products) take effect for the new term
  3. Cisco will notify of any fee changes reasonably in advance of renewal

Section 11

Common Partner Questions

Does the customer need PSTN separately?
Yes. Flex Calling licenses do NOT include PSTN. Options: Cisco Calling Plan (~$3.50/user/mo), Cloud Connected PSTN (third-party), or Local Gateway (customer-owned SBC).
Can a customer mix Professional and Standard licenses?
Yes. You can have Professional and Standard users in the same org. Standard users cannot use shared lines, virtual lines, call queues, or executive/assistant features.
What happens if a customer with EA shrinks below their committed count?
They still pay for the committed count. EA commitments can only go up, never down, during the term.
How does the 15% growth allowance work exactly?
If the customer commits to 1,000 users, they can use up to 1,150 (1,000 + 15%) without triggering True Forward. If they exceed 1,150, a True Forward adjusts them to their actual count at the anniversary. The buffer kicks in after the first 6 months.
Is there a penalty for exceeding the growth buffer?
No penalty. They start paying for the additional users going forward from the anniversary date. Overconsumption during the year is not billed retroactively.
Can we move from Named User to EA mid-term?
Yes. Named User → EA is an allowed upgrade path. The reverse (EA → Named User) is not.
What’s the minimum commitment for Flex Calling?
Named User has no minimum for calling. EA requires 250 Knowledge Workers minimum.
How do Common Area licenses work under EA?
EA includes Common Area licenses equal to 50% of the Knowledge Worker count at no extra charge. Example: 1,000 KW EA = 500 Common Area licenses included.
What if the customer wants to cancel mid-term?
They can’t. The subscription is committed for the full term. They can stop using the licenses, but payment continues.
Does Customer Assist require a separate Calling license?
No. Customer Assist includes a Professional Calling license. It’s not an add-on to Professional — it replaces it at a higher price point.

This guide is based on publicly available Cisco documentation as of March 2026. Partner-confidential pricing, promotions, and ordering details are available through CCW and your Cisco account team.
